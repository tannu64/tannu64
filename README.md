About me
I'm an electrical and communications engineer (Institute of Space Technology, Islamabad) who got pulled into AI automation, not the other way around. I spent years on radar DSP and metamaterials research before n8n and language models rewrote my career path. That background is the lens I bring to every system I build.
I don't do "automations." I build systems that handle real load, real edge cases, and real money on the line. Most automation problems aren't tooling problems, they're architecture problems, and that's the part most builders skip.

How I think about systems
I look for where things will break first. Tight coupling between orchestration and compute. Webhooks with no idempotency. Retry storms from chained nodes nobody traced. Embedding drift in RAG pipelines that goes unmonitored because there's no observability layer. Single points of failure dressed up as "automation."
If you're hiring me, you're hiring someone who will tell you when your stack is wrong, not someone who quietly builds on top of a foundation that's going to collapse at 10x scale.
